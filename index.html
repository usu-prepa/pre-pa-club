Apply to PA School

How to build a strong application

1) Prereqs + planning

  • List programs you’re interested in early.
  • Make a prereq map by semester.
  • Keep a GPA tracker.

2) PCE / HCE hours

  • Track hours weekly (job + volunteering).
  • Know the difference: PCE vs HCE varies by program.
  • Quality + consistency > random hours.

3) Shadowing

  • Shadow multiple specialties if possible.
  • Keep a log: date, PA name, specialty, hours, what you learned.

4) Letters of recommendation

  • Ask early (6–8+ weeks).
  • Give them your resume + draft PS + deadlines.

5) CASPA

  • Order transcripts early.
  • Double-check experiences: dates + hours + supervisor info.
  • Submit sooner rather than later when possible.

6) Personal statement + interviews

  • Answer: Why PA? Why now? Why you?
  • Practice MMI + traditional questions out loud.

Suggested timeline (edit to match your school)

  • Freshman/Sophomore: prereqs + start PCE + join club
  • Junior: ramp PCE + shadowing + start PS drafts
  • Spring before applying: finalize school list, LOR requests
  • CASPA open → submit: transcripts, entries, submit early
